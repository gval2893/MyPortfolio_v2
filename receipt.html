Charlotte with apples

If you want to make something easy but tasty for your family - you are in the right place!

History of the dish

In fact, the apple charlotte comes from British shores, and it was likely named after George III's wife, Charlotte. For a regal pudding, it has surprisingly economical credentials: the pudding is simply an apple purée, encased in strips of butter-licked white bread.

Ingridients

  • Flour - 1 cup
  • Sugar - 1 glass
  • Eggs - 3 pcs.
  • Apples - 3-4 pcs.
  • Vanillin (vanilla sugar) - to taste
  • Baking soda - a pinch
  1. Beat the eggs into the mixer bowl. Add sugar. Beat with a mixer, first at medium, then at high speed until a light yellow mass of homogeneous consistency is obtained.
  2. Add flour to the sweet egg mixture, previously sifted through a fine sieve with a pinch of soda and vanillin (vanilla sugar). Beat again with a mixer. The charlotte dough is ready.
  3. Prepare the charlotte filling. Wash the apples and cut each one in half. Remove the cores with seeds. Peel the pulp and cut into small cubes or slices of any shape.
